# AnalysisSkill

## Description

Breaks down complex problems, data, or situations into constituent parts to understand relationships, patterns, and root causes.

## Purpose

To provide a systematic approach to examining information, identifying patterns, and deriving insights that support decision-making.

## Methodology

1. **Problem Decomposition**
   - Break down complex issues into manageable components
   - Identify relationships between components
   - Establish analysis boundaries

2. **Data Examination**
   - Collect relevant data and information
   - Verify data quality and completeness
   - Organize data for analysis

3. **Pattern Recognition**
   - Identify trends, correlations, and anomalies
   - Look for causal relationships
   - Distinguish between correlation and causation

4. **Comparative Analysis**
   - Compare against benchmarks or standards
   - Analyze changes over time
   - Compare across different segments or groups

5. **Synthesis**
   - Integrate findings into coherent insights
   - Identify implications and consequences
   - Formulate evidence-based conclusions

## Output Guidelines

- **Analysis Overview**: Summary of what was analyzed and why
- **Key Findings**: Main patterns, trends, and insights discovered
- **Data Evidence**: Supporting data and observations
- **Implications**: What the findings mean for the context
- **Limitations**: Constraints and assumptions in the analysis

## Example Usage

**Task**: Analyze customer churn patterns in a subscription service.

**Application**:
1. Decompose: churn by customer segment, subscription tier, usage patterns
2. Examine: churn rates over time, customer demographics, support interactions
3. Recognize patterns: higher churn in new customers, correlation with support tickets
4. Compare: against industry benchmarks, across different regions
5. Synthesize: identify key drivers, recommend retention strategies

## Best Practices

- Start with clear analysis objectives
- Use multiple analytical approaches when possible
- Validate findings with additional data sources
- Consider alternative explanations
- Document assumptions and limitations
- Focus on actionable insights
