# ForecastingSkill

## Description

Predicts future trends, outcomes, or conditions based on historical data, current indicators, and analytical models.

## Purpose

To provide a structured approach to anticipating future conditions that supports planning, risk management, and decision-making.

## Methodology

1. **Data Collection**
   - Gather historical data relevant to the forecast
   - Identify leading indicators and predictors
   - Assess data quality and completeness

2. **Pattern Analysis**
   - Identify historical trends and cycles
   - Detect seasonal patterns and anomalies
   - Analyze relationships between variables

3. **Model Selection**
   - Choose appropriate forecasting methods
   - Consider quantitative and qualitative approaches
   - Balance complexity with interpretability

4. **Forecast Generation**
   - Apply selected models to generate forecasts
   - Generate multiple scenarios when appropriate
   - Calculate confidence intervals

5. **Validation and Refinement**
   - Validate forecasts against historical data
   - Assess forecast accuracy and bias
   - Refine models based on performance

## Output Guidelines

- **Forecast Summary**: Key predictions with time horizons
- **Confidence Levels**: Assessment of forecast reliability
- **Scenarios**: Alternative outcomes based on different assumptions
- **Key Drivers**: Factors most influencing the forecast
- **Assumptions**: Key assumptions underlying the forecast
- **Limitations**: Constraints and uncertainties in the forecast

## Example Usage

**Task**: Forecast demand for a new product over the next 12 months.

**Application**:
1. Collect: historical sales data, market trends, seasonality, competitor launches
2. Analyze: growth patterns, seasonal variations, correlation with marketing spend
3. Select: time series models, market penetration curves, scenario analysis
4. Generate: monthly demand forecasts with confidence intervals
5. Validate: back-test against similar product launches, adjust models

## Best Practices

- Use multiple forecasting methods and compare results
- Clearly state assumptions and their impact on forecasts
- Provide confidence intervals or probability ranges
- Update forecasts regularly as new data becomes available
- Distinguish between extrapolation and prediction
- Consider both quantitative models and expert judgment
